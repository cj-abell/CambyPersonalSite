Career

"I graduated from East Carolina University in 2019 (Go Pirates!) with a Bachelor's of Science in Computer Science. I started my career at nCino, here in Wilmington, NC, as an SDET. I first began working with development teams to help them streamline their testing process to help speed up regression for teams. To do that, we used a Selenium framework written in Java. We would collaborate to decide which manual tests had the most value, as well as which tests were good automation candidates (time-consuming, concise, reliable, etc). These tests included UI, API, and integrations testing. I did that for about two years, then I was shifted onto a team to develop a framework that both the QA Automation team could use, as well as the development teams. The goal was to increase the amount of automated tests being executed, as well as getting automation more embedded into our SDLC. That framework was designed as an npm package, and written using Playwright, Cucumber + Gherkins, written in Typescript."

"After nCino, I joined the team at FedEx Ground. I was put on a team that helped with automating new and existing web applications that the teams were building. That included re-writing older tests written in Protractor, as well as fleshing out additional testing functionality . "

Hi, I'm Camby Abell, an SDET/SWE from Wilmington, NC.

I have been an SDET for four years, and I absolutely love doing it. I discovered my affinity for automated testing in college during my studies. We had a class project where we had to effectively emulate a modern software company. We had to have "standups", meetings, sprints, retros, etc. and one of the requirements was testing. It could have been manual tests, unit tests, or automated tests. I had never heard of automated testing before this. So I took on the task of automated testing and immediately was intrigued by what could be done using the tools. Once I discovered it could be an actual career, I knew the path I wanted to be on. It perfectly mixes my love for software engineering, and my curiousity when testing software.

Some of my favorite things to do are play golf, do brain teasers (NYT Crossword, Wordle, Immaculate Grid), spend time outdoors(especially at the beach), and watching baseball. Shoot me a message on LinkedIn and we can nerd out over some baseball statistics!
